I am an engineer with a primary interest in software, computer science, cloud / web computing products and associated technologies. If you've seen my education history, you may have noticed I have two degrees that aren't really related to computer science. For reasons that even I don't know I did a weird transition in college from Electrical Engineering / Computer Science to Biomedical Engineering to Neuroscience, which I then followed up with a Masters in biomedical engineering. Though these were the official paths of study I took, I had always maintained some sort relationship with technologies as indicated by my primary fields of research, in my undergraduate years this was on the utilization of computer vision to determine better statistical data from animal behavior tests and later on for my research thesis I did work with medical device imaging algorithms for blood flow mapping. In 2012, I was given the opportunity to take a software engineering position at a Defense company. While I have enjoyed the opportunity, the methodologies, technologies, and practices employed are out-of-date from a software perspective. The company inherently designs combat vehicle systems and thus takes a very long cycle systems engineering approach to its product development. And while I have tried my best to elevate and improve efficiencies with current standards of software development within the software groups, I have been mostly met by apathy, disinterest, or resistance to anything which strays from legacy practices. Still, in the absence of a hard order to stop trying to rock the boat, I've developed a lot of skills across the software product development cycle with wearing many hats as a Software Developer / Tester (QA) / Project Manager (Agile Engineering PM) / Release Engineer / Operations (VCS, Continuous Integration, Testing and Deployment). I'm hoping to make a change to a new company that produces software products and has a culture committed to continuous software improvement.
